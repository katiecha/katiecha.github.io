What is an occupational heat-related illness?

üìö Occupational heat-related illnesses (OHRIs) occur when the body cannot control, and more specifically lower, internal temperatures, after being exposed to hot outdoor or indoor working environments.

Common OHRIs include heat exhaustion, heat stroke, and heat syncope.

üìó Heat exhaustion is characterized by large amounts of sweating, water loss, and related bodily responses.
üìï Heat stroke is the inability to control body temperature. This is the most severe HRI and can result in death.
